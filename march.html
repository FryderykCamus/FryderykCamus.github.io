Less Squares

Post by Jonah Smith, Mar 13, 2017.

  • It would seem logical that the quickest and most efficient way to win a game of battleships is to select squares on a certain diagonal. The smallest ship is two square and so even though only squares diagonally connected would be selected no ship would be missed. Consequently, the number of squares available I halved. Following this logic, I implemented a very small module which simply restricts the computers shots to square on the same diagonal. To determine whether a square apart of this set I simply drew up a grid. One can then see that squares of the diagonal I selected were limited to either both even x and y coordinates, or odd x and y coordinates. Consequently the module was simple in producing.
